Strategic Context
An estimated $30B+ of gold moves through illicit or informal channels annually. Eight major corridors account for the bulk of this flow. Each is driven by a distinct mechanism: duty arbitrage, sanctions evasion, conflict financing, or hawala remittance. The India–Gulf corridor is the richest data node; the others represent equally urgent global intelligence gaps.

The Provenance Problem
Gold is unique among commodities — once refined, its origin cannot be determined by any physical test. An estimated 20-30% of formal supply originates from conflict zones. The LBMA Responsible Gold Guidance, OECD Due Diligence Guidance, and EU CSRD all require origin documentation — but no single public platform has aggregated this intelligence globally until now.

The Dubai Nexus — How Conflict Gold Enters the Formal Market
TraCCC GMU
Step 1 Artisanal miners in DRC/Mali/Burkina Faso sell at below-market prices to local traders controlled by armed groups
Step 2 Gold transported overland to coastal ports, often via multiple transit countries to obscure origin
Step 3 Arrives at UAE (primarily Dubai) gold souks and refineries, declared as "recycled" or "scrap" gold
Step 4 Refined and re-exported as Dubai-origin gold with DMCC certification, laundered into formal supply chain
Step 5 Enters global supply to LBMA refiners, jewellery manufacturers, ETFs — origin effectively untraceable

The Russia Gold Evasion Case Study
After SWIFT sanctions were imposed in February 2022, Russia rapidly pivoted to gold as a sanctions circumvention mechanism. Russia exported an estimated $10B+ of gold to UAE in 2022 alone, according to BIS and Reuters analysis. Turkey absorbed additional flows. The US Treasury issued secondary sanctions warnings to UAE and Turkish entities in 2023. Hong Kong emerged as a secondary absorption node. This single corridor represents the largest documented use of gold for sanctions evasion in history — and demonstrates precisely why supply chain intelligence is now a national security priority for G7 governments.
